# UK Hair Transplant Co

## Business Context

- UK Hair Transplant Co is a standalone acquisition and guidance brand created by Meditr.
- It must not publicly state or imply an organic connection to Liv Harley Street Hospital.
- Public positioning can reference work with `a premium clinic in central London` or `at the heart of London`.
- The current real clinic relationship is singular, even if the public brand is designed to expand to more UK and Turkey clinics later.

## Core Positioning

- Premium London hair transplant guidance with stronger clinical standards and clearer decision support.
- Turkish hair restoration expertise is a trust and capability signal, not a budget story.
- London convenience, local aftercare, and clearer standards should be the main public advantages.
- Avoid anti-Turkey framing because the brand may add Turkey pathways later.

## Trust Language

- Use accurate wording around `GMC-registered doctors` and `CQC-registered providers in England`.
- Do not say `GMC-approved`, `CQC-approved surgeon`, or equivalent endorsement language.
- Do not overclaim expertise levels such as `best`, `leading`, `top`, or `specialist` unless substantiated.
- The safest recurring frame is: stronger standards, regulated environment, realistic planning, aftercare clarity.

## Commercial Constraints

- Do not build the whole brand around a fixed headline price.
- Price messaging should be transparent, quote-driver-led, and flexible enough for future clinic expansion.
- The site should not read like a generic referral agency or comparison site.
- The site should feel like a premium decision platform with medical/cosmetic-treatment seriousness.

## SEO and Content Priorities

- Core commercial pages: London, cost, female, service pages.
- Core decision pages: UK vs Turkey, how it works, FUE vs DHI, without shaving.
- Core trust pages: standards, clinic selection, patient guidance.
- Core moat pages: recovery timeline, side effects, donor/recovery-related guidance.

## Audience

- Audience is heavily male, but women must be handled as a serious, visible pathway.
- Traffic is likely mobile-first and iPhone-heavy.
- Tone should be calm, premium, adult, clinical, and direct.
- Avoid laddish, cheap, hype-heavy, or overtly “luxury sales” language.

## Design Priorities

- The site should feel premium, clinically serious, and editorially confident rather than spa-like, bargain-led, or overly corporate.
- Avoid generic healthcare templates, purple-on-white SaaS styling, and black-and-gold “luxury reveal” clichés unless they are used with restraint.
- Typography should feel intentional and adult, with strong hierarchy and mobile readability.
- Design should support both male-majority demand and female credibility without splitting into two conflicting visual systems.
- Trust content should be visually prominent near the hero and near decision points, not buried deep in the page.
- Mobile Safari and iPhone behavior should be treated as first-class constraints for layout, tap targets, sticky actions, and forms.

## Conversion Approach

- Primary CTA should usually be an assessment/consultation request.
- Secondary CTAs should route users into standards, cost, recovery, and method content.
- Copy should address suitability, planning, trust, and recovery before pushing the conversion.

## Measurement Priorities

- Track form starts, form submissions, click-to-call, WhatsApp starts, and calendar/booking clicks as distinct lead actions.
- Separate soft leads from qualified consultations and patient-ready handoffs where possible.
- Preserve UTMs and landing-page attribution through the enquiry flow.
- Judge SEO and CRO changes on lead quality, consultation quality, and patient potential, not traffic alone.

## Paid Search Priorities

- Google Ads should compete on trust, suitability, London convenience, and transparent planning rather than a race to the bottom on price.
- Avoid ad messaging that depends on a fixed hero price or anti-Turkey framing.
- Prefer high-intent search coverage first: London, cost, female, recovery-aware, and method-aware queries.
- Expect strong mobile traffic and make click-to-call and WhatsApp paths measurable.
- Capture identifiers needed for paid attribution and offline qualification where possible.
